Youve made it this far? Wow.
2015: Before it was cool, I set out to make a quad copter on an interns budget. I used PlayStation
controllers, Arduino, cheap transmitters, and eBay for parts.
2016: I set out to build an wireless camera gimball (worked ok, 3D printed parts and used servos for
motion) and a robot that would scan for colored cubes within a certain perimeter for my micro-controllers
class.
2017-2018: I built a dining table out of a very nice walnut "cookie" slab that was 100+ years old. That
was my rst woodworking project, learned a ton about nishes for wood (and how to avoid condensation
from cold glasses from marking your table), hand tools and power tools specically for woodworking, the
art of sanding and grits, epoxy llings, wood swelling, and crack growth. Seats 4.
2018-2019: My second woodworking project was an "entryway organizer". Mail, keys, coat, and mini-
malist design. I used geometry to wedge the mail against the wall, magnets to hold the keys, and brass
to hold it together and hang the coats. Along the way, building and acquiring the proper tools to make
this was probably the most challenging, especially in a Seattle apartment.
2018: Restored a really old and rusty jointer, took forever. Worked great.
2020: Im currently working on a CNC router, its mostly made out of 80/20. Will update once its
nished, but my goal is to be able to amplify my ability to build in order to unhinge my designs. I
would like to experiment more with bio-mimicry, and although not impossible to do with hand tools (and
potentially more rewarding), the level of detail a CNC oers is un-paralleled for the time invested (both
on the learning curve and recurring side).
